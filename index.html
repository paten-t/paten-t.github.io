Paten't — a world sans patents

The term patent originates from the Latin word patere, which means to stand open.

Yet, patent systems are not as open as they could be; rather, stifling progress and limiting the collective advancement of knowledge and our modern society.

Long overdue, patents must be relegated to the past.

Patent Reform

The patent system was designed millennia ago[1]. They were not prepared for the modern era.

Urgent global challenges, such as climate change, pandemics, and food security, require rapid and widespread innovation.

To follow our long-term trajectory as a human civilization, it is necessary to speed up the process of invention and relegate the slow and problematic patent system to the past.

This project proposes a policy that would address many of the issues with the current patent system and simultaneously add further benefits, while not creating new ones.

Unlike past proposals to modernize the patent system, this project aims for a complete reimagination of the way we manage inventions, in a broad stroke to address many of the problems that face patents.
