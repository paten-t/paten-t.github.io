Detail #4a — Enforcement of the monopoly.

If a two-thirds majority of protection holders agree that a third party is exploiting the invention without having won the bid, they can complain to the WIMP court — similar to how a patent monopoly is enforced. The WIMP court will judge infringement based on the details specified by the inventor. If the complaint succeeds, the protection holders share the amount awarded. However, in the case of failure, only those who voted to litigate will share the cost.
