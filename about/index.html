About this Project

Beginnings

This project was started and nurtured with the Non-Trivial Fellowship.

Motivation

I've grown up in a developed country shaped by innovation, which has led to a relatively prosperous life that I have been able to have.

  • Yet, these same factors, which drove this innovation, can also impede the development of developing countries. As much as I take 21st century technology for granted, others do not, and it is important that innovation is equitable.
  • Furthermore, if it is true that patents are not productive in encouraging innovation, even in well-off countries, then, for selfish reasons, a new system is desired that will do so. This project, if carried out to its conclusion, would allow myself and my posterity to live the best possible lives.

The Long-Term

How is this project impactful to our long-term future?

As has often been said, this is a critical time for all of humanity. Living a minute and a half from midnight (according to the Doomsday Clock), we need innovation to pull us away from the issues that plague us, and immediate innovation not tied up with patents and legal issues is pertinent to our success in solving our problems. In addition, the future that lies before us is dependent on the progress we make today. Reforming the way we handle inventions will be the backbone supporting everything else.
