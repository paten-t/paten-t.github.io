Idea #5

Transition procedure.

  • Previous

  • Comment

Switching from patents to WIMP will be difficult, but it may be done, albeit quite slowly (perhaps half a century).

Details

Details delve further into each idea.

Detail #5a — Raising of patent application fees.

The first step is to make patents less appealing by asking patent offices to raise the application fees extremely gradually. However, current patents can continue and expire without impediment or additional costs, and patents can continue to be filed for some time.

Detail #5b — Introduction of the WIMP.

Simultaneously, the WIMP platform will enter as an alternative after extensive testing. All businesses and individuals can automatically register on the WIMP platform through contacting their respective patent offices, who will be equipped to handle this. Eventually, because of the benefits associated with this new platform, as well as the artificial price-raising of patents, only the WIMP platform will be left as a viable alternative.

Detail #5c — Mathematical model of patent price raising.

Hyperbolic growth could be a good model for raising the prices of patents.

  • After the first year, the price of patent prosecution is multiplied by x, where x>1.
  • Then, after the first (1 year)/x, the price is again multiplied by x.
  • Then, after the first (1 year)/(x2), the price is multiplied by x for a third time.
  • This repeated multiplication continues until, eventually, the price grows to infinity (called a singularity).
    • For example, if x=1.02, i.e., the price increases by 2% each interval, but these intervals decrease by 2% each time, it would take over half a century (51 years) before the price explodes to infinity.
This model would also give businesses a clear timeline before they would need to transition onto the WIMP platform, as the singularity is a hard deadline.
